Leh Ladak The Thiksey Monastery is located in the Leh district of Ladakh, India

It is a prominent Tibetan Buddhist monastery and belongs to the Gelugpa sect. The region of Leh-Ladakh is known for its stunning, barren landscapes, high-altitude mountains, and rich Buddhist heritage. Prayer flags, like those seen outside Thiksey Monastery, are an integral part of Tibetan culture. They symbolize the spread of goodwill, compassion, and prayers for the well-being of all sentient beings. The flags are often placed in high places to allow the wind to carry the prayers across the land..

Kerala | Culture The tradition of Kerala women wearing red and white dresses

In Kerala, women wear red and white dresses, often seen in the traditional Kasavu saree during festivals like Onam. The red and white colors symbolize purity, celebration, and auspiciousness. This elegant attire reflects Kerala's rich cultural heritage and is worn during religious ceremonies and special occasions.

Built by Maharaja Man Singh I in the 16th century, it combines Hindu and Mughal architecture. Key features include the Sheesh Mahal, Ganesh Pol, and expansive courtyards. It served as the main residence of the Rajput rulers.

Taj hotel | Mumbai The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel in Mumbai

Opened in 1903, it was designed by architect Sitaram Khandekar in a blend of Indo-Saracenic and Victorian styles. Overlooking the Arabian Sea, the hotel has been a symbol of elegance and hospitality. It played a key role in Mumbai’s history, especially during the 2008 terrorist attacks.
